Why Universal Design?

Universal design makes the web fair, usable, and accessible for everyone — not just people with perfect vision, fast devices, or ideal conditions.

1. It's a Justice Issue

People experience disability in permanent, temporary, or situational ways. Designing universally respects everyone’s right to access information.

2. It's Good UX

Clear navigation, readable text, and predictable behavior help all users — not just those with disabilities.

3. It Future-Proofs Your Work

Semantic, accessible code lasts longer and is easier to maintain, update, and scale.

4. It's the Next Step for the Web

Movements like web standards, responsive design, and semantic HTML shaped the web. Universal design is the next evolution.
